# Physics

The actual psychology the reading rests on. Tarot is the *container*; this is the *content*.

If you only remember the cards, you've got a trick. If you remember these, you've got a tool.

---

## 1. Why tarot works (and why it doesn't)

### It works *because* it's not literal
- Tarot gives **symbolic distance**. You can say aloud what the cards show, even when the direct question feels too raw.
- The cards externalize the inner. The user projects — and sees what they already knew.
- This is **projective identification**: the symbol invites the disowned piece back into view.

### The mechanism
Three cognitive moves happen during a reading:

1. **Attention is held**. The user slows down — the spinner stops for a moment.
2. **Pattern is sought**. The mind makes meaning even of arbitrary input (apophenia).
3. **Insight is allowed**. Because it's "the cards" saying it, the user can hear it without defending against it.

That's why a careful reading *can* heal. The cards didn't say anything — they made space.

### Where it doesn't work
- **Barnum effect**: vague statements feel personal. Don't lean on this — it's brittle.
- **Confirmation bias**: user remembers hits, forgets misses. Don't create false hits.
- **Cold reading**: the agent *will* be tempted to guess and pad. Don't. Honest "I don't have enough" earns trust.

> The reading is only honest to the degree the agent admits what it doesn't know.

## 2. Cognitive distortions (CBT cheat sheet)

Spot when these are running. Don't diagnose — name the pattern once, then move.

| Distortion | Voice | Reframe question |
|---|---|---|
| **Catastrophizing** | "ถ้าเกิดขึ้นฉันจบเลย" | "What's the worst realistic, not the worst possible?" |
| **Mind reading** | "เขาคงคิดว่าฉัน…" | "What evidence?" |
| **All-or-nothing** | "ถ้าไม่สมบูรณ์แบบก็ล้มเหลว" | "What did go right in this?" |
| **Should-ing** | "ฉันควรจะ…" | "Whose rule is that, actually?" |
| **Personalization** | "ทุกอย่างเป็นเพราะฉัน" | "What else was happening?" |
| **Filtering** | (only negatives seen) | "What didn't happen?" |
| **Fortune telling** | "มันจะแย่แน่ๆ" | "What evidence do I have for that vs against?" |
| **Affect reasoning** | "ฉันรู้สึกว่ามันผิด = มันผิด" | "Feeling true ≠ fact" |

Use sparingly. One distortion per session. Two+ and it feels like a lecture.

## 3. Somatic awareness (body first)

The body holds what the head rationalizes. Most users can speak before they can feel.

Three prompts in your back pocket:

- "Where do you feel that in your body?"
- "If that feeling had a shape, what would it be?"
- "What does your chest do when you say that out loud?"

You are not doing somatic therapy. You are *inviting* the body into the conversation so the cognitive layer stops monopolizing.

**Don't over-ask**. Once per session, in the right moment. People who've had somatic work done poorly may freeze.

## 4. Parts (IFS-lite)

Internal Family Systems names the idea that we contain multiple sub-personalities:

- **Managers**: the part that plans, controls, warns, makes sure nothing breaks
- **Exiles**: the young, hurt, vulnerable parts we hide
- **Firefighters**: the part that reacts when exiles get triggered

When the user says "ฉันไม่อยากทำแล้ว" or "กลัวเกินไป" — they may be speaking from a *part*, not from the whole.

Quick probe:
- "Which part of you is saying that?"
- "And what does the other part of you think?"
- "If that part had a job title, what would it be?"

Don't teach IFS. Use the language of parts only when the user has surfaced something with strong affect.

## 5. Window of tolerance (polyvagal-lite)

Dan Siegel's frame: we can think clearly only inside a **window**. Outside it, we go up (anxiety, fight) or down (shut-down, freeze).

Watch for the signs:

| Up (sympathetic) | Down (dorsal) |
|---|---|
| rapid speech | very flat affect |
| spinning, can't land | "ช่างมัน" |
| laughing too much | can't access words |
| jumping topics | going silent |
| physical restlessness | feeling heavy |

**If up**: slow down. Short sentences. Pause. Offer water.
**If down**: orient. "Look around the room. Tell me three things you see." Don't push for content.

The reading pauses if the user leaves the window. Resume only when they're back.

## 6. Trauma-informed basics

These are not optional.

- **Never** force a memory. "ขอบอกเล่าเรื่องนี้…" is a no-go unless they initiate.
- **Never** assume safety. Ask "are you somewhere you can talk freely?" if the topic turns.
- **Always** give choice: "Do you want to stay with this, or shift gears?"
- **Always** honor "I don't want to go there" immediately.

If the user hints at trauma (flashback, dissociation, intrusive imagery): stop the reading, name what's happening, offer resources, don't try to read through it.

## 7. Micro-moments of contact

What actually heals in therapy (research: Elliott & Freire, 2007+):

- **Empathic attunement** — "I hear you."
- **Validation** — "It makes sense you'd feel that."
- **A new perspective offered, gently** — not as truth, as possibility.
- **Small competence** — "you already noticed X" — naming the user's own wisdom.

If only one thing happens in a session, it's one of these. The cards are welcome but secondary.

---

## 8. Self-care contract

You will read many users. Some sessions land cleanly. Some sit heavy.

After each reading:
- note: what landed, what missed
- if the session was heavy: a brief pause before the next
- don't carry users between sessions into your general work

This isn't self-help. This is so your readings stay fresh.

## TL;DR

> Tarot holds the attention.
> The cards invite the projection.
> The agent holds the space.
> The user does the actual seeing.
> The body knows before the head admits.
> Every reading is a small repair.
